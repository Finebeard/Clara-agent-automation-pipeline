Demo Call Transcript - Acme HVAC

Agent: Thank you for calling Acme HVAC Services. How can I help you today?
Caller: Hi, I'm just trying to understand how you handle calls.

Owner: Sure. Our company name is Acme HVAC Services.
Owner: Our business hours are Monday through Friday, 8 AM to 5 PM Eastern Time.
Owner: We are usually closed on weekends except for true emergencies.

Owner: Our office address is 123 Main Street, Springfield, New York 10001.

Owner: We support residential and light commercial HVAC service, including:
- furnace repair
- air conditioning repair
- seasonal maintenance
- new unit installation

Owner: For us, an "emergency" means:
- no heat when the outside temperature is below 45 degrees
- gas smell
- sparking or smoke coming from equipment

Owner: Emergency routing works like this:
- During business hours, emergency calls should be transferred directly to the on-call technician's mobile at 555-111-2222.
- After hours, emergency calls should first try that same technician number.

Owner: Non-emergency routing:
- During business hours, if the office line is available, transfer to the main office line at 555-000-1234.
- If no one answers, take a message and summarize the issue.
- After hours, always take a message for non-emergencies and mark it as next-business-day follow-up.

Owner: Call transfer rules:
- Always announce the caller's name, phone number, and brief issue when transferring.
- If the transfer fails or no one picks up within 30 seconds, return to the caller.

Owner: Integration constraints:
- We currently do not use a CRM.
- Please do NOT integrate with any external ticketing tools.
- Just log the call summary in your own system and send us a daily email summary manually for now.

Owner: Office-hours flow summary:
- Greet the caller, confirm how you can help, collect their name and phone number, determine if it is an emergency, and then either transfer or take a detailed message.

Owner: After-hours flow summary:
- Greet the caller, specify that they have reached the after-hours answering service, confirm if it is an emergency, and follow the emergency vs non-emergency routing rules.

Owner: That's basically how we handle calls today.
